Tax & GST Guide

Understanding Your Tax Obligations

Even if you're new to business finance, here's what you need to know — in plain language.

GST
What is GST and Who Needs to File It?
Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a unified indirect tax on supply of goods and services. Any business with an annual turnover above the threshold must register and file GST returns regularly. We handle your entire GST compliance from registration to monthly/quarterly filings.
ITC
Input Tax Credit — Don't Leave Money on the Table
When you pay GST on your purchases, you can claim that amount back against your outgoing GST. This is called Input Tax Credit (ITC). Many businesses miss out on thousands in refunds because their records aren't in order. We ensure every eligible credit is claimed.
TDS
TDS — Tax Deducted at Source
If your business makes payments above certain thresholds (rent, contractor fees, professional charges), you're required to deduct TDS and deposit it with the government. Failure to do so attracts interest and penalties. We track every TDS liability and ensure timely deposits.
DBL
Avoiding Double Taxation
Double taxation occurs when the same income is taxed more than once — either at the business and personal level, or across multiple jurisdictions. We structure your accounts correctly and apply appropriate tax treaties and exemptions so you never pay more than legally required.
P&L
Reading Your Profit & Loss Statement
Your P&L shows Revenue − Expenses = Net Profit (or Loss). It answers: Is my business making money? Which costs are too high? Can I afford to expand? We create your P&L every month and walk you through what it means in simple terms.
B/S
Understanding Your Balance Sheet
A balance sheet shows what your business owns (assets) vs what it owes (liabilities). The difference is your net worth. It's essential for loan applications and shows investors and banks the financial strength of your business at a specific point in time.
